Abstract

Large Language Models (LLMs) have transformed software development, enabling the creation of AI‚Äëpowered applications known as LLM‚Äëbased agents. A recent trend brings these capabilities to smartphones through mobile AI agents, which can autonomously perform complex tasks directly on the device. However, the privacy and security threats of such agents remain largely unexplored. In this paper, we take an initial step toward a systematic analysis of these risks, focusing on prompt injection attacks.

We consider threat models specific to mobile deployments, particularly vulnerabilities introduced by common operational practices such as using screenshots for perception and external APIs for control. Drawing on our experience deploying agents such as AppAgentX and mobile-agent-e within the AndroidWorld environment, we identify seven potential attack vectors and discuss practical challenges, including system protections and prompt adherence when executing complex attacker‚Äëdefined tasks. Our findings refine the threat model for mobile AI agents and highlight the importance of developing more secure, self‚Äëcontained on-device agents that reduce the broad attack surfaces inherent in current screenshot‚Äë and API‚Äëbased designs.
